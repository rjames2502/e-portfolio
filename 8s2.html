** SUMMARY POST**

After reviewing the posts of my peers, there is common agreement, that it would be unethical to change any values, in order to make the report fit better, the agenda of the commissioning company. The American Statistical Association (ASA, 2022) have put together a set of guidelines to help statistical practitioners make decisions ethically, and to provide guidance how to act ethically. Principle H , in the ASA guidelines, is particularly relevant in this case study, as it refers to potential misconduct that could occur if the original data values are changed or omitted from the study. Louch et al (Louch et al, 2020) also state in their study, that it “it is therefore vital to be able to look at data, its analysis, and its presentation, using an ethical framework”. Once the practice of altering data being submitted, becomes acceptable or common, there can be no trust afforded to any statistical data, as the accuracy and honesty that it refers to, would always be questionable at best, or not trusted at all in a worst-case scenario, and therefore rendering it useless to most people.
