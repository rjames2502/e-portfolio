Peer Response #2

Peer Response was in regards to Initial Posting from Pearce Begley -

**Link to Initial Post by Pearce Begley**

Hi Pearce, I agree wholeheartedly with your summation, that it is possible to produce “a desired outcome” by simply being selective over the data considered during any research program. It is essential that all relevant and available data is considered, regardless of what the pre-conceived desired results are. Your further points linking heart disease and other ailments, to sugars is also interesting to note. It was observed (Indrayan, 2018) that it only takes research results to be wrong by 1 percent, for there to be significant risk to many thousands of people.

References: Indrayan, Abhaya. (2018). Statistical fallacies & errors can also jeopordize life & health of many. Available from: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6396556/. [Accessed 8 October 2023].
