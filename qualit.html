Assessment - Security and Risk Management Collaboration Discussion 1 - Summary

My Summary response

In my initial post, I described how the digitization of legacy processes, into Industry 4.0, is changing the way that big industry performs and manufactures products. This movement, has many benefits such as reduced cost and optimization, but there are drawbacks, such as reduced requirement for human staffing, which could drastically alter the employment pool in the manufacturing area. From reading through responses from my peers, it is clear that they see Industry 4.0 as an evolution which has both benefits and drawbacks to its evolution. The advantages of Industry 4.0, include increased productivity, easier expansion and costs to name a few, all of which can help to enable growth and efficiency (machinemetrics, 2018), lightsondata (Lightsondata, ND). Benefits for a business are clear, but the negatives are less well publicized. With a new modernized manufacturing process, the human impact would be significant. As I mentioned in my original post, there would be a significant requirement for retraining of existing staff, or employment of new, more technically aware people. The level of technical ability in staff will increase greatly over the majority of staff employed today, which in itself, could be quite difficult and costly to achieve. Inevitably, there will be a large number of people currently employed, that are no longer needed due to automation or lack of required skills. With the new concept being “online all the time”, there is a perhaps not new, but significantly increased risk of Cyber-attack. Though not really a business, Iran found out the hard way, what the risks of being connected really are (nordvpn, 2022). The Stuxnet virus caused chaos in their nuclear facilities, but was designed to be very specific in the damage that it caused, and only affected Windows machines with connections to Siemens PLCs (manufacturing machines). Progress and change are inevitable, but it is essential that as the industry moves to a new model, that the cyber security and processes implemented, are maintained and adjusted continuously. Vigilance will become the “key word” for Cyber Security within the Industry 4.0 model.
