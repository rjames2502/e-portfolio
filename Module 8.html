Unit 1: Scientific Investigations and Ethics

							
The module began with a review of Ethics in Computing.
One thing to be clear from the outset, is that Ethics and Legality, are not always aligned,
and this may lead to less than desirable situations.
Topics such as Data Protection Regulations, may help to protect somewhat the end users, however,
with regulations differing somewhat between countries, and in some countries like the Us, even
within the different States themselves, there is no guarantee of this.

    Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

							
There was a decent amount of background reading associated with this unit, mostly associated with the process 
behind writing a good literature review.
This in itself was invaluable reading, as it helped solidify the necessary processes and planning
that are required before the review itself is attempted.
In the provided literature, there were tables and charts to aid in the preparation.
I found this aspect to be quite interesting. 
Prior to reading through all of the documentation, I had assumed that I would have a good grasp of
what needs to be done to achieve a good grade, but after review, I think that there would have been
gaps in my approach.
What I find particularly useful, is the feeling that this whole module is setting us up to do well
in the final Projet Module that follows.
There is a lot of material that follows the process past the Literature Review and onto the full planning methodology 
for the Project which is definately invalauble for the understanding.
The project Module is quite a daunting prospect, so I am happy with the setup of this module.
