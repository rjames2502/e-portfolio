** SUMMARY POST**

After reading through all of the different responses, it is clear that the topic of Ethics in Computing is important, and should not be disregarded or ignored. In all responses to the different case studies, Ethics plays a pivotal role in fairness and good treatment, but in some cases, there are complications due to Legal responsibility differing from that of the Ethical responsibility. In my particular case study – Web-Hosting but without any Cyber protections being put in place by the company – Rogue Services, they are legally providing a service with the bare minimum of effort on their part, exposing both the companies paying for the service, and any customers using it, to possible cyber-attacks. In this particular case, Ethically, it can be said that Rogue Services is providing the services in an un-ethical manner. Like with the published BCS Code of Ethics (BCS, 2022), Formosa et al (Formosa et al, 2021) also propose implementing a number of cybersecurity ethical principles, whilst maintaining balance and common sense in the way that the proposed ethical code is implemented. The IEEE (IEEE, 2016) layout a strategy, where the Computer Professions themselves, also need to make themselves aware of Ethical and Legal issues, so it is clearly an accepted standpoint from Professionals, to deploy any services to the best of your ability, with all possible mitigations in place. However, without there being also necessary legal framework in place, that makes it nationally/internationally a requirement to implement these policies, it is unlikely that all companies or entities will follow the guidelines voluntarily.

References: BCS, (2022). Code of Conduct for BCS Members. Available from: https://www.bcs.org/media/2211/bcs-code-of-conduct.pdf [Accessed 25 August 2023]. Formosa, Paul. Wilson, Michael. Richards, Deborah. (2021). A principlist framework for cybersecurity ethics. Available from: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0167404821002066 [Accessed 25 August 2023]. IEEE, (2016). Cyber Security Education and Law. Available from: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/7515415 [Accessed 25 August 2023].
