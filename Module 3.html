Module 3 - Security and Risk Management

Security and Risk Management 
This module will focus on the increasing requirement to pair Security and risk together in modern business. 
The module will cover a broad range of THREAT Models that are available, qualitative and quantitative assessments, 
as well as descibing how to perform and carry out appropriate assessments.
As part of this module, it is required to work in small groups, in order to perform assessments of a business
and the risks associated with digitization and additionally becoming an online business.

The module is run over 12 weeks, split into 12 units.

Personal REFLECTIONS of the module will be recorded at the end of this page
Unit 1: An Introduction to Security and Risk Management 
"Risk management is the process of identifying risk, assessing risk, and taking steps to reduce risk to an acceptable level". 
The module begins by explaining why risk needs to be continually assessed, and how these assessments can help tailor the defences
appropriately to protect an Enterprise.

It can be split into 3 parts - 
1) Determine the different risks and vulnerabilities that may be presented, and the likelihood of them occurring.
2) Evaluate and prioritize the risk and establish mitigation processes/techniques against them.
3) Continual evaluation and assessment, so as things change, the mitigation can be adapted.

The unit continues to describe different methodologies for risk assessment, and shows where it can sometimes
go wrong during assessment. 
The favoured method uses quantitive analysis methods, as opposed to qualititive, as they are less subjective
and should be more accurate to define.

Industry 4.0 is introduced, as a method to ditigize traditional manufacturing processe in order to make them more efficient.
