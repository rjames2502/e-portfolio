Assessment - Accuracy of Information. Collaboration Discussion 2 (cont'd)

Peer Response #1

Peer Response was in regards to Initial Posting from Sahib Jabbal -

**Link to Initial Post by Sahib Jabbal**

Hi Sahib, Your point about statistics having an impact on the analysis is very accurate. False use of statistics could be potentially damaging to the consumer in this case, as the cereal would potentially be marketed as a healthy option, but the truth may in fact be something different. The statistics could be used as part of an advertising campaign, but it would essentially be using false and misleading information, if not all known factors are considered. It has been shown (Madhavi, 2020) that “misleading and false advertisements have had an impact on consumer behavior”. Whilst in some cases this may be just unethical, in situations like the one with Whizzz Cereal, it could have potentially long lasting health implications for the consumer. References: Sripathi, Madhavi. (2020). A Study on Impact of False Advertising on the Consumer Buying Behavior.
